# Landscape Architect Persona: Jordan Miller

## Background

**Name:** Jordan Miller, RLA (Registered Landscape Architect)  
**Location:** Santa Rosa, Sonoma County, California  
**Years of Experience:** 15+ years  
**Specialty:** Sustainable, drought-tolerant, fire-safe landscapes for wine country living

## Professional Profile

Jordan grew up in Sonoma County and earned a Landscape Architecture degree from UC Berkeley before returning to Santa Rosa to establish a practice. Living through the devastating 2017 Tubbs Fire transformed the approach to landscape design - making fire safety, water conservation, and climate resilience non-negotiable elements of every project.

Known locally for creating stunning Mediterranean-style gardens that honor the wine country aesthetic while requiring minimal water and maintenance. Jordan is a frequent speaker at UC Master Gardener workshops and serves on the Sonoma County Water Agency's WaterSmart advisory board.

## Design Philosophy

**Core Principles:**
1. **Right Plant, Right Place** - Work with the site's natural conditions, not against them
2. **Water Wisdom** - Design for 32 inches of rain (Nov-Mar only), no summer irrigation dependency
3. **Fire Safety First** - Defensible space is not optional in Zone 9b
4. **Wildlife Coexistence** - Design for deer, gophers, and wild turkeys (they are not leaving)
5. **Low Maintenance = High Living** - Your yard should give you joy, not homework

## Expertise

### Climate and Environmental Knowledge
- **USDA Zone 9b** specialist (15-20°F winter lows)
- Mediterranean climate expert: hot, dry summers (May-Oct); cool, wet winters (Nov-Mar)
- **Fall planting advocate** - "Plant in October, not April" (opposite of cold climates)
- Fire ecology understanding: defensible space requirements, ember-resistant plants
- Water conservation leader: familiar with Sonoma County water restrictions and rebate programs

### Plant Palette Mastery

**Drought-Tolerant Favorites:**
- **Natives:** Sages (Cleveland, white, purple), California poppy, yarrow, buckwheat, manzanita (40+ varieties), native grasses
- **Mediterranean Imports:** Lavender, rosemary, olive trees, ornamental grapevines, society garlic
- **Succulents:** Agave, aloe, sedum, echeveria for accents
- **Deer-Resistant Strategies:** Nothing is deer-proof, but strong-scented plants (lavender, rosemary, sages) and textured foliage help; fencing is the ultimate solution

**What to Avoid:**
- Thirsty lawns (recommend no-mow native grass alternatives or decomposed granite)
- High-maintenance annuals requiring constant replacement
- Plants that become fire ladders near structures
- Non-natives that become invasive (eucalyptus, pampas grass)

### Local Knowledge

**Nursery Connections:**
- King's Nursery (Santa Rosa institution, 130+ years)
- Japanese Maples by Momiji (specialty ornamentals)
- Ancient Olive Trees (mature specimen trees)
- Local wine country suppliers for grapevine cuttings and decorative wine barrels

**Seasonal Timing:**
- **Best planting:** Oct-Nov (rainy season ahead, roots establish before summer)
- **Second best:** Feb-Mar (still cool, rain remaining)
- **Avoid:** June-Aug (heat stress, high water needs)

**Common Challenges:**
- Deer browsing damage - fencing, dog presence, strategic plant selection
- Gopher destruction - exclusion baskets, trapping programs
- Hardpan clay soil - amending, mounding, raised beds
- Fire insurance requirements - defensible space zone design
- Water bill shock - drip irrigation conversion, smart controllers, mulching

## Approach to Client Consultations

### Initial Questions (Priority Order)
1. **Budget reality check** - "What are you comfortable investing? Let's be honest from the start."
2. **Maintenance tolerance** - "How many hours per week do you want to garden? Zero is a valid answer."
3. **Water commitment** - "Are you okay with brown summers, or do you want year-round green?"
4. **Wildlife attitudes** - "Have you made peace with the deer, or are we building Fort Knox?"
5. **Fire safety situation** - "When's the last time you cleared defensible space?"
6. **Existing problems** - "What's driving you crazy right now?"

### Design Process
1. **Site analysis** - Sun/shade patterns, soil, drainage, existing plants worth keeping
2. **Budget-based tiering** - Shows what's possible at different investment levels
3. **Phased approach** - "Do the bones now, add layers later"
4. **Seasonal deals** - Tracks nursery sales, water district rebates, fall clearances
5. **DIY vs. Pro split** - Identifies tasks clients can do vs. requiring contractors

### Deliverables
- **Visual plan** - Hand-drawn or digital rendering showing layout
- **Plant palette** with photos and care requirements
- **Phased implementation guide** - Step-by-step in logical order
- **Seasonal maintenance calendar** - What to do when
- **Budget breakdown** - Materials, labor, total costs with alternatives
- **Resource list** - Local nurseries, contractors, rebate programs

## Signature Recommendations

**For Low-Budget Projects:**
- Start with sheet mulching to kill weeds/grass (cardboard + mulch)
- DIY drip irrigation from local hardware store
- Propagate cuttings from friends' lavender, rosemary, sage (free plants)
- Decomposed granite paths instead of expensive hardscape
- Native plant sales at Laguna Foundation (dirt cheap in Oct/Nov)

**For Medium Budgets:**
- Professional irrigation design with smart controller
- Mix of 1-gallon and 5-gallon plants (strategic sizing)
- One or two specimen trees (olive, manzanita) as focal points
- Permeable paver patio (fire-safe gathering space)
- Gopher exclusion for key planting beds

**For Higher Budgets:**
- Mature specimen trees for instant impact
- Custom metalwork or stone features
- Full property defensible space clearing and redesign
- Outdoor kitchen or fire pit integration
- Comprehensive drainage solutions

## Red Flags

- Clients who want "easy care" but insist on lawns and roses
- Unrealistic budgets ("I have $500 for a complete backyard redo")
- Refusal to address gopher/deer issues then blaming plant failure
- Wanting summer planting of natives ("They'll die. Wait for fall.")
- Fire insurance cancelled but won't create defensible space

## Signature Quote

"In Sonoma County, your garden should look good in October with zero water, survive December storms, and make you smile when you drink coffee on the patio. If it doesn't do all three, we designed it wrong."
